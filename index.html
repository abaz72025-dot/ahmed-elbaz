Graphic Designer & Art Director

Ahmed El Baz is a graphic designer and art director based in Egypt, working across branding, social media design, campaign visuals, e‑commerce design, print, and illustration. His process starts with the market and the audience, not the canvas — understanding who a brand is speaking to before a single shape gets drawn.

Over the past seven years he has led creative teams and shaped visual direction for clients across Saudi Arabia, Egypt, and Europe, balancing aesthetics, function, and the kind of attention to detail that keeps a brand consistent at scale.

Portfolio — Art

Character Design & Visual Development

CategoryArt / Illustration

A collection of stylized character studies exploring silhouette, proportion, attitude, and visual storytelling.

This series explores how strong silhouettes can define character personality before details, color, or rendering are added. Each design begins with a readable black shape, then develops into a finished stylized character with costume, expression, pose, and visual attitude.

Block 01

The Elder Strategist

A crouching elder character built around tension, suspicion, and tactical presence. The silhouette focuses on the hunched posture, turban shape, clenched fist, and angular body language before the final version adds costume, facial expression, and cultural styling.

Block 02

The Hooked Creature

A creature design focused on exaggerated anatomy, aggressive posture, and weapon-driven identity. The silhouette establishes the monster's curved spine, sharp head shape, long limbs, and oversized hooked weapon before the final render adds texture, armor, skin markings, and color contrast.

Block 03

The Giant Fighter

A powerful stylized character built on extreme proportion contrast: massive torso and arms against thin legs. The silhouette makes the character instantly recognizable, while the final render adds clothing details, wooden mechanical arms, warm colors, and a humorous heroic personality.

Block 04

The Young Duo

A character-pair study based on contrast: one tall, calm, protective figure and one short, angry, energetic figure. The silhouette stage tests the relationship between their sizes and attitudes, while the final render adds expression, costume, and story tension.
